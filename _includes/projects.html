Research project: Association of responce to treatment of rheumatoid arthritis with Somalogic proteins.

Context

Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) is an autoimmune disease treated with drugs that are commonly split into two classes: conventional synthetic disease-modifying anti-rheumatic drugs (csDMARDs) and tumour necrosis factor (TNF) α inhibitors (anti-TNFs). Patients react differently to these drugs with a proportion of patients exhibiting resistance to either csDMARDs, anti-TNFs or both.

Aim

I explore the effect of genetic loci of various proteins in human plasma (pQTLs) on the response to csDMARDs and anti-TNFs. Since proteins are common drug targets, such knowledge may improve the existing treatment of RA or lead to the development of new treatments.

Method

I connect the genotypes and measurements of the response to treatment in cohorts of patients with RA collected by MATURA consortium with the largest to-date atlas of human plasma pQTLs (Sun et al., 2018).

I use the GENOSCORES platform to aggregate and weight the genotypes of patients with RA by the reported effects of pQTLs to obtain genotypic scores. I then study the associations of these genotypic scores with the response to treatment. These associations are informative of any pQTLs affecting the efficiency of either csDMARDs or anti-TNFs.

GENOSCORES project

I develop and maintain the GENOSCORES platform.

GENOSCORES is a curated database of published genome-wide association studies (MySQL), a web platform (PHP, Laravel) and an R package along with a suite of helper tools (various languages) linked together with an API.

GENOSCORES is used with any individual-level dataset consisting of genome-wide SNP genotypes to calculate genotypic scores for the phenotypes stored in the database.

GENOSCORES server and database

I maintain the GENOSCORES server, develop and maintain the GENOSCORES database.

GENOSCORES is a curated database of published genome-wide association studies with >1M phenotypes. I maintain both the server and the database, import new GWAS and support users.
